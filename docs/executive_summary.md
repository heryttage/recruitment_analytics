# Executive Summary — Recruitment Funnel Review

**Prepared for:** Head of People, Helios Technologies
**Prepared by:** People Analytics
**Period analysed:** January 2024 – December 2025 (1,800 applications)

## The situation

Recruiting spend (~$4.6M annually) has outpaced headcount growth. Leadership wanted to know whether the money is well spent, where the funnel leaks, and whether hiring is fast enough.

## What the data shows

**Conversion.** Of 1,800 applications, 9.4% (169) resulted in a hire. The largest single drop-off is the very first step: 42% of applicants are rejected at recruiter screening — more than at any later stage. Most of that low-fit volume arrives through job boards.

**Channel value.** The channels diverge sharply once you compare hire rate against cost:

- **Employee referrals** are the standout — a 15.5% hire rate at **$6,959 per hire**.
- **Recruitment agencies** cost **$63,136 per hire**, nearly 9× referrals, and their offers are declined more often.
- **Job boards** generate the most applications but the weakest yield (5.4%) at $48,115 per hire.

**Speed and cost.** Median time-to-hire is 46 days (90th percentile 60 days), consistent across departments — pointing to process rather than any one team. Blended cost-per-hire is $27,432.

**Assessment.** The skills test is predictive: hire rate rises steadily with score band, validating it as an early filter.

## Recommendations (in priority order)

1. **Build a referral engine.** Reallocate budget from job boards into a referral incentive. Referrals are both cheaper and higher-converting.
2. **Automate top-of-funnel screening** for job-board applicants to cut the 42% manual rejection load and free recruiter capacity.
3. **Restrict agencies** to senior/lead roles open beyond 45 days; renegotiate fees.
4. **Adopt formal SLAs** — 46-day target, escalate at 60.

## Estimated impact

Shifting even 10% of job-board spend to referrals would lower blended cost-per-hire while improving hire quality, and earlier automated screening would return recruiter hours to higher-value pipeline work. These are directional estimates intended to frame a budget conversation, not a committed forecast.
